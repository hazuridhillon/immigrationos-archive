# ImmigrationOS — A Public Archive

A fully sourced, publicly accessible research archive documenting the architecture, history, capabilities, contracts, and civil liberties implications of ICE's AI-powered deportation platform, built by Palantir Technologies.

**Live site:** [hazuridhillon.github.io/immigrationos-archive](https://hazuridhillon.github.io/immigrationos-archive)

---

## What This Is

This archive traces the 14-year, $287 million relationship between U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) and Palantir Technologies — from the original 2011 Homeland Security Investigations contract through the 2025 deployment of ImmigrationOS and its field-facing targeting tool, ELITE.

The site was built as a public interest resource, drawing on government procurement records, investigative journalism, civil liberties organization research, congressional correspondence, and primary company documents.

---

## What's Inside the Archive

- **Overview** — What ImmigrationOS is, how it works, and why it matters
- **Timeline** — Annotated history of the ICE–Palantir relationship from 2011 to 2026
- **The Three Pillars** — Targeting & Enforcement Prioritization, Self-Deportation Tracking, Immigration Lifecycle Operations
- **System Architecture** — Cross-agency data fusion, ELITE field targeting, ICM backbone, deportation logistics
- **Civil Liberties Concerns** — Fourth Amendment issues, healthcare data surveillance, vendor lock-in, algorithmic bias, conflict of interest, human rights due diligence
- **27-Source Reference Table** — Every source linked, color-coded by type (Gov, Press, NGO, Company, Congress, Academic)
